UNIT 6 🌡️

Climate Change Master Notes — 1.5°C Limit, Carbon Sink, Net Zero, Climate Justice & NCAP

🌡️ 1. Importance of 1.5°C and 2°C Temperature Limit

Scientists believe that if global temperature rises above 1.5°C–2°C compared to pre-industrial levels, climate impacts become very dangerous.

✅ Below 1.5°C — Safer Future

  • Less extreme weather
  • Protected coastal areas
  • Better food & water security

❌ Above 2°C — Extreme Risks

  • Severe heat waves
  • Melting glaciers + sea rise
  • Biodiversity collapse
  • Food & water crises
🎯 Likely Exam Question
Why is limiting global warming to 1.5°C important?
Limiting warming to 1.5°C reduces extreme weather events, protects ecosystems, decreases sea-level rise, and improves food and water security for all nations.

⚖️ 5. Climate Justice

Definition: The idea that climate change disproportionately affects poor and developing countries, even though they contribute less to pollution.

😢 Who Suffers More?

  • Poor & developing nations
  • Small island nations (sea rise)
  • Agricultural communities (drought)

✅ Principle of Justice

  • Big polluters must take greater responsibility
  • Developed nations provide climate finance
  • Technology transfer to developing nations

Environmental Management Master Notes — Articles, ISO 14001, LCA, Circular Economy, EIA

📜 1. Article 48A vs Article 51A(g)

🏛️ Article 48A — Government Duty

Directive Principles of State Policy

"The State shall protect and improve the environment and safeguard forests and wildlife."

👤 Article 51A(g) — Citizen Duty

Fundamental Duties of Citizens

Every citizen must protect forests, lakes, rivers, and wildlife.

48A → Government Protects 🏛️  |  51A(g) → Citizen Protects 👤
