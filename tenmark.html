🎯 Sample 10-Mark Answer
Q: Explain Net Zero and Carbon Sink.
Carbon Sink: A carbon sink is anything that absorbs more CO₂ than it releases. Examples: Forests, Oceans, Wetlands, and Mangroves. They help reduce global warming by storing carbon naturally.

Net Zero: Net Zero is a state where the amount of greenhouse gases emitted equals the amount removed from the atmosphere. Formula: Carbon Emitted = Carbon Removed. Methods to achieve Net Zero include renewable energy, afforestation, and carbon capture technology. India's target is Net Zero by 2070.
🎯 Sample 10-Mark Answer
Q: Why is limiting global warming to 1.5°C important?
Limiting warming to 1.5°C is important because: (1) It reduces the frequency and intensity of extreme weather events like floods, droughts, and heat waves. (2) It protects coastal areas from sea-level rise. (3) It preserves ecosystems and biodiversity from collapse. (4) It improves food and water security for billions of people. (5) It reduces the risk of irreversible climate tipping points. The Paris Agreement (2015) sets this target as the global goal.
